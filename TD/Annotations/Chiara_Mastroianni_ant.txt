Chiara Charlotte <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiara_Mastroianni">Mastroianni</entity> (born 28 May 1972) is a French-Italian actress and singer. <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiara_Mastroianni">She </entity>is the daughter of Marcello <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiara_Mastroianni">Mastroianni</entity> and Catherine Deneuve.

Career

<entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiara_Mastroianni">She </entity>earned a César Award nomination for her first feature film appearance in André Téchiné's My Favorite Season (1993), starring her mother in the lead role. The next year<entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiara_Mastroianni"> she </entity>appeared in Robert Altman's Prêt-à-Porter. Her first starring role came in 1995, opposite Melvil Poupaud in Le Journal du séducteur, a romantic comedy inspired by "The Seducer's Diary" chapter from Soren Kierkegaard's first major text Either/Or. <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiara_Mastroianni">She </entity>worked with Paupaud again in Three Lives and Only One Death (1996), playing the daughter to her real-life father, and in Time Regained (1999), also starring her mother.

Along with a few supporting performances in French films,<entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiara_Mastroianni"> she </entity>appeared in Gregg Araki's Nowhere (1997) and took time off to have her first child with sculptor Pierre Thoretton. <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiara_Mastroianni">She </entity>returned to filmmaking to star in the leading role of Manoel de Oliveira's The Letter (1999), based on the novel La Princesse de Clèves by Madame de La Fayette. After appearances in Mike Figgis' Hotel (2001) and shooting in Italy for Le parole di mio padre (2001),<entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiara_Mastroianni"> she </entity>played the lead in Delphine Gleize's interconnected drama Carnages (2002), a winner at several international festivals. In 2003,<entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiara_Mastroianni"> she </entity>starred in the comedy drama Il est plus facile pour un chameau....
Personal life

<entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiara_Mastroianni">Mastroianni</entity> has two children: son Milo (b. 31 December 1996) with ex-boyfriend Pierre Thoretton and daughter Anna (b. 22 April 2003) from her former marriage to musician Benjamin Biolay.[1]
